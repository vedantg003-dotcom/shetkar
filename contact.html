Find answers to common questions about fertilizers, seeds, and our products.

Urea should be applied during the vegetative growth stage of crops. The best time is before irrigation or rainfall for maximum absorption. For most crops, apply during active growth periods - typically 3-4 weeks after sowing. Split applications (2-3 doses) are recommended for better results. Avoid applying during flowering or fruiting stages.
The recommended dosage of DAP varies by crop: Cotton: 50-60 kg/acre, Soybean: 40-50 kg/acre, Wheat: 50-60 kg/acre, Vegetables: 40-60 kg/acre. Apply DAP at the time of sowing as basal dose. It provides both nitrogen and phosphorus essential for early root development. For precise recommendations based on your soil type, consult our experts.
Urea (46-0-0) is a nitrogen-only fertilizer that promotes vegetative (leaf and stem) growth. DAP (18-46-0) provides both nitrogen and phosphorus - phosphorus is essential for root development, flowering, and fruiting. Urea is typically applied during active growth, while DAP is applied at sowing time. Both are important for crop nutrition but serve different purposes.

Common signs of micronutrient deficiency include: Yellowing between leaf veins (Iron/Manganese deficiency), Brown or purple leaf edges (Phosphorus/Potassium), Stunted growth (Zinc deficiency), Poor flowering and fruiting (Boron deficiency). If you notice unusual symptoms in your crops, bring a sample to our store or share photos via WhatsApp for expert diagnosis and product recommendations.
Cotton crop protection involves multiple products for different pests: For bollworms - use recommended insecticides at ETL (Economic Threshold Level), For sucking pests - neem-based or systemic insecticides work well, For fungal diseases - preventive fungicide sprays during humid conditions. We stock a complete range of cotton protection products. Consult our experts for a customized spray schedule.
